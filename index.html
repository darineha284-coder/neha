About Cyber Fraud

Cyber Fraud covers scams and crimes that target users and organisations through digital channels. This Website explains common fraud types, how to spot them, and what to do if you or someone you know is targeted.

Types of Cyber Fraud

Common frauds you should know about

Phishing

Fraudulent emails or message that trick you into giving personal information or clicking malicious links.

Identity Theft

Attackers steal personal data to impersonate you and commit fraud or open accounts.

Malware

Software installed without consent to spy, steal data or lock devices (ransomware).

Online Scams

Fake marketplaces, cryptocurrency scams, romance scams, job scams and more.

How to Stay Safe

Actionable steps you can take right now.

  1. Use strong, unique passwords. Use a password manager to generate and store them.
  2. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA). Prefer app-based 2FA or hardware keys where possible.
  3. Think before you click. Inspect sender addresses and don't open unexpected attachments.
  4. Back up important files. keep offline or cloud backups to recover from ransmware.
  5. Report scams. Tell parents/teachers and report to your local authority or platform.
